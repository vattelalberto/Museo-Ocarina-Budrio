Ocarina Museum

Budrio is known as the homeland of ocarina. In this museum you will discover how it was born and how it spread around the world.

The Ocarina and terracotta musical instruments Museum in Budrio was opened in 1994.
"Budrio Ocarina Club", whose members were parents of pupils at the ocarina school and people who had interest in the local tradition, had been collecting antique ocarinas, photographs and historical material concerning the ocarina since 1978 on the premises of the school in Via Golinelli, taking care of the exhibits and creating in this way what was to become the nucleus of the present day museum.
The exhibits were for the most part donations made by citizens of Budrio and have been added to over the years by purchases made by the town council and by donations from ocarina makers, particularly from abroad: the museum was founded to preserve and promote a local cultural tradition which would otherwise have been lost.

The new displays on show in the museum today present former traditional ocarina makers from Budrio and other areas on the ground floor, whereas contemporary craftsmen from all over the world are presented on the floor above. There is also a very attractive and colourful collection of traditional folk whistles and other terracotta instruments which are mainly percussion. The museum displays a selection of the most important items in its possession. Other material is kept in the archives for use in temporary exhibitions and is available on request for study by students and those interested in the subject.
