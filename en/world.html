Ocarina around the World

Born in Budrio mid nineteenth century, the ocarina is nowadays spread all over the world. The first ocarina maker which was not from Budrio was Einrich Fiehn. In the late nineteenth century already his black-painted ocarinas with golden borders spread all around the world, in particular in the USA.

Europe

Outside of Budrio, various manufacturers are active in Italy and Europe: Austria (where the ocarina is commonly used in popular music)), Germany, France, Spain, Romania, Scandinavia, etc. In many cases the construction of ocarinas is combined with that of traditional clay whistles.
In Great Britain a type of egg-shaped ocarina with 4 or 6 holes is very common (even in schools), invented by the musician John Taylor in the 60s inspired by South American globular flutes.

Asia

The ocarina arrived in Japan in the 1930s. Aketagava began to build ocarinas and in the 1960s, in collaboration with musician and composer Kayama, invented the 12-hole ocarina model.
Following the success of the ocarinist Sojiro, a pupil of Kayama, the ocarina became very popular in Japan in the mid-1980s. Other manufacturers were born and a model of multi-chamber ocarina was developed (ie: various ocarinas fused together to increase the range of the instrument).

Today the ocarina is also very popular in South Korea, Taiwan and China, where it is played by millions of children in many schools. 90% of the production and the world market is in Asia.

America

In the USA the ocarina is quite popular among young people, also thanks to the success of the video game The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time by Nintendo.

In Latin America (Chile, Peru, Mexico, Central America) the zoomorphic and anthropomorphic globular flutes have an ancient tradition that continues today.
