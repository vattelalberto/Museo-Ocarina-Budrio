Ocarina Museum

The Ocarina and terracotta musical instruments Museum (unique in the world) illustrates the evolution of this particular musical instrument born in Budrio, as well as its construction techniques and traditional musical repertoires. It displays a wide collection of ancient and modern manufacts and a rich documentary, bibliographical and audio apparatus.

The Museum is named after Franco Ferri, founder of the Municipal Ocarina School - along with other Budrio inhabitants - and lifelong promoter and collector of instruments and documents about the history of ocarina.
